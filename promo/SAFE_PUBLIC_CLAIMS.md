## DO NOT CLAIM

Avoid these — they are unverified, misleading, or likely to backfire:

1. **"X clients served" or "Y projects completed"** — don't cite specific client counts or revenue figures without hard data to back it up
2. **"Fastest portfolio site" or "perfect Lighthouse score"** — don't claim performance rankings without a current, reproducible benchmark
3. **"Enterprise-grade" or "used by Fortune 500 companies"** — vague credibility language that sounds hollow without specifics
4. **"Thousands of visitors" or traffic metrics** — don't cite analytics numbers unless they're real, current, and you're comfortable making them public
5. **That the site is currently live at a specific domain** — only include a live URL if it's actually deployed and you intend to maintain it
